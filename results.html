What do these results mean?

There are five independent axes - Government, Civil Liberties, Economy, Foreign Relations, and Culture - Each has two opposing values assigned to them. They are:

Democracy (Government)
Those with higher Democracy scores believe citizens should have a right to determine the policies or members of their government.

Dictatorship (Government)
Those with higher Dictatorship scores believe governance should be left in the hands of professionals who know what they are doing rather than the public

Liberty (Civil liberties)
Those with higher Liberty scores believe that the free will of individuals should be protected from the interests of the state
They tend to support legalization of guns/drugs, privacy, free speech, decentralized education, and consent between the state and individuals

Security (Civil liberties)
Those with higher Security scores believe that individuals should sacrifice their own liberties for the collective wellbeing of the state
They tend to support law & order, surveillance, censorship, centralized education, and social obligation such as mandatory vaccination or military service.

Competition (Economy)
Those with higher Competition scores believe in an unrestricted economy where hard-work and good ideas result in higher profits
They tend to support low taxes, deregulation, private business, free markets, and class mobility

Equality (Economy)
Those with higher Equality scores believe in a restricted economy where the wealthy must sacrifice profit-potential to provide for their workers and consumers
They tend to support high taxes, regulation, public services, unions, and redistribution of wealth.

Globe (Foreign Relations)
Those with higher Globe scores believe their country should collaborate with others to benefit the world as a whole.
They tend to support loose borders, small military, free trade, global cooperation, and peaceful relations.

Nation (Foreign Relations)
Those with higher Nation scores believe their country should pursue their own interests, even at the expense of other nations.
They tend to support strong borders, strong military, protectionism, national independance, and imperialism.

Progress (Culture)
Those with higher Progress scores believe their society should be open and tolerant to new ideas and other cultures.
They tend to support multiculturalism, LGBT, rehabilitative justice, pro-choice, atheism, and a warm compassionate citizenry.

Tradition (Culture)
Those with higher Tradition scores believe their society should maintain a strong coherent culture.
They tend to support monoculturalism, family values, punitive justice, pro-life, religion, and a strong thick-skinned citizenry
